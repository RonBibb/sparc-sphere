Single smooth equilibrium halo profiles (NFW, Burkert, Einasto) leave statistically significant, radially organized residuals in ~35% of SPARC rotation curves: a minimum-order radial-domain decomposition, with model order selected by the Bayesian Information Criterion, requires a second cored radial domain in that fraction of galaxies. Such a detection could reflect the flexibility of the fitting basis or a localized systematic in one velocity-field extraction rather than a property of the galaxies. We report the most direct test of galaxy-intrinsic origin: whether the multi-domain selection reproduces when the rotation curve is re-derived from the original radio data cubes by independent software, holding the baryonic model fixed so that only the measured velocities change. It does. In four of five adjudicable multi-domain systems the selection recovers — decisively for NGC 2403 (ΔBIC = 121, on a curve derived from different observations than the SPARC curve) and NGC 3198 (ΔBIC = 55), on a warp-aware curve for NGC 2841, and at lower significance on a different-telescope extraction for NGC 3741 — with the crossover radius preserved. The one non-reproduction and one power-limited case are reported explicitly, and single-domain controls reproduce their single-domain selection. Because a rotation curve constrains only the enclosed-mass profile M(<r), we make no claim about the three-dimensional geometry or physical origin of the structure; the result is confined to a feature in the enclosed-mass profile that is reproducible under independent velocity derivation. A companion analysis (Bibb 2026b) presents the full method-independence and robustness program that further characterizes it.
